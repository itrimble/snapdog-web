SnapDog is a native SwiftUI screen-capture app for macOS 14 (Sonoma) and later — capture, record, annotate, and ship to 19 destinations without leaving your menu bar. Launching soon on the Mac App Store.

Capture

Fullscreen, window, region — plus the modes Screenshot.app never learned.

Fullscreen, window & region

ScreenCaptureKit-powered captures bound to ⌃⇧1 / ⌃⇧2 / ⌃⇧3 out of the box — every shortcut is rebindable in Settings → Hotkeys.

Region loupe & size badge

Drag with a 4× magnifier loupe, a live pixel-coordinate readout, and a w × h size badge as you go. Space toggles the loupe; Esc cancels.

A window picker that respects z-order

Click-to-pick overlay with hover highlight and an app + title badge under the cursor. The top window wins even when frames overlap — and modal dialogs and floating panels are pickable, not just normal windows.

Take over ⇧⌘3 / ⇧⌘4 / ⇧⌘5

Optionally bind SnapDog's Fullscreen, Region, and Window captures to the system screenshot shortcuts — offered during onboarding, with a warning when a combo clashes with a reserved macOS shortcut.

Delayed capture

Capture menu → Delay 3s / 5s / 10s with a floating countdown HUD. Esc cancels the countdown.

Display under your cursor

Capture whichever display the cursor is on instead of always grabbing the primary — and the region picker overlays every connected display, so multi-monitor setups just work.

Stitch capture

Pick a region once, scroll at your own pace, click Add Frame — SnapDog aligns each new frame against the last and appends only the non-overlapping content into one tall image.

Recapture Last

One optional global hotkey re-runs whichever capture kind you used most recently — no picker UI, no menu trip.

Native shutter sound

The genuine macOS screen-capture shutter plays the moment a capture lands, just like Screenshot.app. On by default; toggle it in Settings → General.

Record

Screen recording built on ScreenCaptureKit — straight to MP4, MOV, or GIF.

MP4 / MOV / GIF

Pick GIF as a first-class recording format — SnapDog records to a temp MP4 and finalizes the GIF on stop. Any saved recording can also be exported to GIF later from History.

System audio + microphone

System audio is covered by the Screen Recording permission; the mic records as its own AAC track with a separate toggle and permission flow.

Webcam PiP — even on region recordings

Picture-in-picture on fullscreen, window, and region recordings. The floating preview panel is draggable and resizable, and the saved file composites your camera exactly where the panel sat. FaceTime-style mirror toggle included.

Webcam-only & one-shot overrides

Record just the camera with Webcam Only, or use the Screen + Camera submenu to override the webcam setting for a single recording without flipping the global toggle.

Pause & resume

⌘⇧P pauses mid-recording; presentation timestamps are adjusted on resume so the saved file plays back with no gap.

Click ripples

Yellow ripples expand at every left and right click while recording — baked into the saved file, perfect for tutorials. Toggle in Settings.

Quality presets & countdown

High / Balanced / Small presets or a custom-bitrate stepper for both screen and webcam tracks, plus a cancellable 3-2-1 countdown HUD before recording starts.

Multi-monitor aware

A display picker appears before each recording on multi-monitor Macs (auto-skipped with one display), and the floating recording HUD excludes itself from the captured stream.

Edit

A full annotation editor, one keystroke away — no round-trip to Preview.

Every annotation tool

Rectangle, rounded rectangle, ellipse, triangle, diamond, pentagon, hexagon, and star in a shapes gallery — plus reversible arrows, lines, freehand, click-to-add-vertex polygons, text, and fillable speech balloons.

Redact & emphasize

Blur and pixelate for secrets, a highlighter for the important bits, auto-incrementing numbered step badges, and a spotlight that dims everything except the region you choose.

Objects panel

A layers-style list of every annotation on the canvas — select, reorder, and delete without hunting for tiny handles.

Edit anything, after the fact

Select any existing annotation and change its color, size, fill, and opacity. Fills come in Outline, Solid, or Translucent; Shift constrains lines to straight angles.

Undo, zoom, duplicate

Snapshot-based Undo (⌘Z) and Redo (⌘⇧Z), Duplicate (⌘D), pinch to zoom, Option-drag to pan, ⌘0 to reset, ⌘= / ⌘− to step.

Crop, straighten, resize

Crop keeps surviving annotations in place by translating them into the new coordinate space; a fine-angle straighten slider auto-crops the result; resize to exact pixel dimensions.

Watermark on the canvas

Move and resize your watermark directly on the image — changes flow back into the global Effects settings. Any system font, custom color, size, opacity, and free X/Y placement.

Export your way

Save as PNG, JPEG, TIFF, or HEIC with a quality control. Open Image and Save panels default to your capture folder, and an eyedropper picks colors straight off the image.

Text & Tools

Vision-powered utilities, each one a menu item away.

Recognize Text (OCR)

Draw a region and Apple's Vision framework reads it on-device, landing the text on your clipboard. Optionally route OCR through your configured AI provider for stylized fonts and mixed-language content.

QR — decode & generate

Decode QR, Aztec, DataMatrix, and PDF417 codes from the screen, the editor, or any History row. Generate a 512×512 QR PNG from any text, straight to the clipboard.

Pick Color

Single-pixel screen sampler with a magnifier loupe and live hex / RGB readout. Click to copy the hex.

Measure with Ruler

Drag a measurement line over a frozen screen with live length, Δx, Δy, and angle. Shift snaps to horizontal or vertical.

On-device transcription

Recordings transcribe locally — Apple SpeechAnalyzer on macOS 26+, SFSpeechRecognizer before that — so audio never leaves your Mac. Auto-transcribe after each recording if you like; transcribed rows get a badge, and transcripts can be summarized via your AI provider.

AI Analyze — ready on first launch

Describe a capture, extract text, or suggest a filename with the Built-In Demo (On-Device), which requires no account, key, model download, or network. Optional cloud and locally hosted providers remain available in Settings.

Automate & Share

The ShareX pipeline, rebuilt native for the Mac.

Visual workflow builder

Chain Screenshot → OCR → AI Summarize → Action as cards in Settings → Workflows. Data flows between steps: OCR text becomes the Slack note, and an upload step's URL threads into the downstream webhook.

Actions after every capture

A reorderable list of post-capture automations — upload, webhook, Slack, AI — governed by one master mode: Save Locally, Run Actions, or Ask Every Time with a floating per-capture chooser.

19 upload destinations

Imgur, Dropbox, Google Drive, OneDrive, Amazon S3 and S3-compatibles (R2, B2, MinIO, Spaces), SFTP, Slack, Discord, Telegram, Notion, Mastodon, Bluesky, Cloudinary, GitHub, iCloud Drive, AirDrop, Catbox.moe, 0x0.st — each with a per-destination Test button.

ShareX .sxcu import

Drag in any existing ShareX custom-uploader config — multipart, form-urlencoded, JSON, or binary, with {response} and {json:dot.path} URL templates. URL shorteners import the same way and auto-chain after upload.

Signed webhooks

POST a JSON payload on Capture Saved or Capture Uploaded with HMAC-SHA256 signing (the same header scheme as Slack, GitHub, and Stripe) and exponential-backoff retry. Works with Zapier, n8n, and Make.com.

Capture-preset hotkeys

Bind whole capture chains to global hotkeys — each with its own capture kind and per-workflow clipboard / save / editor / pin / beautify / upload flags that bypass the global settings.

History that pulls its weight

Search filenames and tags, filter by source, mark favorites, Quick Look with Space, open any capture straight into the editor, copy as Markdown / HTML / BBCode, and export a self-contained HTML gallery.

Watch folders & Pin to Screen

Auto-import new images and videos from any folder into History, keep the last five captures in a Recent menu, and pin any capture as a floating thumbnail you can drag straight into Slack, Mail, or Finder.

Private by design

Screenshots are sensitive. SnapDog treats them that way.

On-device by default

OCR runs on Apple's Vision framework and transcription runs on Apple's speech frameworks — locally. Nothing leaves your Mac unless you configure a destination or an AI provider yourself.

Optional providers, direct traffic

The Built-In Demo stays on your Mac. If you select another provider, requests go directly from SnapDog to that provider; SnapDog operates no intermediary AI service.

Secrets live in the Keychain

API keys, OAuth tokens, SFTP passwords and private keys, and webhook signing secrets are all stored in the macOS Keychain — never in plain-text preferences.

Sandboxed, modern auth

App Sandbox stays on in release builds. OAuth runs through ASWebAuthenticationSession with PKCE — no bundled client secrets — and SFTP pins host keys on first use.

Native & localized

A SwiftUI menu-bar app for macOS 14 (Sonoma) and later — no Dock icon, no Electron — localized into 13 languages, from Arabic to Traditional Chinese.
